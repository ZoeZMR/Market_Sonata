# Artistic Statement

## Market Sonata

*A generative music system translating financial markets into musical narratives.*

---

### Why turn financial data into music?

A stock chart and a piece of music are both **records of collective human
feeling unfolding in time.** A market is millions of people expressing hope,
fear, greed and doubt through the only vocabulary a market allows — buying and
selling. The resulting line on a chart is an emotional seismograph. We already
describe it in emotional language: markets *panic*, they *rally*, they *recover*,
they are *nervous* or *euphoric*.

Music is the oldest technology we have for storing and transmitting emotion
across time. So the premise of *Market Sonata* is simple and, I think,
inevitable: if a market is already an emotional record, it should be possible to
**re-encode that emotion into the medium built to carry it.**

This project is not an attempt to "sonify data" as a novelty, nor to predict
prices, nor to trade. It treats the market as a *libretto* — a source of
dramatic structure — and asks what it sounds like when a piano tells that story
with intention.

### The central claim

> Financial markets and musical compositions are both **time-series of human
> emotion.** Market Sonata is a translation instrument between the two.

A rising market is not merely "up." It has the emotional grammar of hope: a
melody that climbs, a harmony that resolves toward the major, a texture that
opens up. A crash is not merely "down." It is grief and shock: an inverted
theme, dissonance, a collapse of register. By committing to these mappings
consistently, the piece earns the right to be *heard as meaning something*.

### Why a piano, and why these composers?

The work lives deliberately in the world of **Joe Hisaishi, Philip Glass and
Max Richter** — minimalism, contemporary classical and film scoring. That
tradition is built on exactly the tools a market needs:

- **Repetition and gradual process** (Glass) mirror how markets grind and
  trend — small changes accumulating into large ones.
- **Melodic warmth over an ostinato** (Hisaishi) lets a single human theme sing
  above mechanical motion, just as a personal story rides on top of impersonal
  market forces.
- **Restraint and emotional patience** (Richter) let silence and sustain carry
  weight, so that volatility, when it comes, actually lands.

Solo piano keeps the work **intimate and human.** The market is vast and
faceless; the piano is one person at one instrument. That tension — the personal
voice interpreting the impersonal system — *is* the piece.

### What I want a listener to feel

That they are hearing a *portrait*, not a readout. That the AAPL of 2020 and the
AAPL of 2022 are different people with different stories. That data, so often
used to flatten human experience into numbers, can be turned back around and
made to sing.

— *Market Sonata* is, finally, an argument that **technology can be an
instrument of empathy**: a way of listening to systems we usually only measure.
